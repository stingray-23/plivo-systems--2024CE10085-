Architecture & Design Overview

To meet the strict real-time, low-latency constraints dictated by the 20ms frame clock, standard ARQ feedback (NACKs) was discarded. This prevents the severe latency inflation caused by Round-Trip Time (RTT) negotiations across a hostile network. Instead, a transport-layer Forward Error Correction (FEC) protocol was successfully implemented.

  • Sender (Block-XOR): The sender organically groups inbound frames into pairs. It transmits Frame 0 (164 bytes) and Frame 1 (164 bytes) sequentially, immediately followed by a redundant FEC packet (164 bytes). This FEC packet contains the XOR of the two previous payloads. The total overhead is bounded statically at 1.54x, safely passing the strict 2.0x requirement.
  • Receiver (Jitter Buffer): The receiver allocates a flat array mapping to act as a resilient jitter buffer, inherently resolving network reordering via direct index mapping. If a packet is lost in transit, it utilizes the surviving peer packet and the XOR packet to regenerate the lost payload instantly without network blocking. A precision select() timeout loop strictly syncs playout with the harness deadline schedule.
