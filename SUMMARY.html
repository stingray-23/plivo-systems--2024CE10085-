Architecture & Design Overview

To meet the strict real-time, low-latency constraints dictated by the 20 ms frame clock, standard ARQ feedback (NACKs) was avoided because retransmissions incur additional RTT over a hostile network. Instead, a transport-layer Forward Error Correction (FEC) scheme was implemented.

  • Sender (Block-XOR FEC): Frames are grouped into pairs. Two original packets are transmitted followed by one XOR parity packet, allowing recovery from a single packet loss within the block while maintaining approximately 1.54× bandwidth overhead.
  • Receiver (Jitter Buffer): A sequence-indexed receive buffer handles packet reordering and duplicate suppression. Missing packets are recovered using XOR parity whenever possible, and playout is synchronized with the harness deadlines using a select()-based timing loop.
